Occupied by Absence - Fotoreeks

My photography is about capturing moments where stories linger — even in silence and stillness. With this series, I was drawn to the quiet tension between abandonment and life. These buildings may be empty of people, but they still hold traces of presence, memories, and subtle movements that hint at what once was, or what might still be.

I’m fascinated by the contrast between decay and resilience: how nature slowly reclaims spaces, how light and shadow play on forgotten walls, and how small details can tell a bigger story. Through my lens, I try to reveal the invisible life in these forgotten places, inviting viewers to look closer and feel the atmosphere that hangs in the air.

Photography, for me, is a way to pause, observe, and connect with spaces that are often overlooked — to find beauty in the quiet and the imperfect.
